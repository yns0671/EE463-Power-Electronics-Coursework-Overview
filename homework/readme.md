# Homework Assignments – EE463 Power Electronics I

This folder contains selected homework reports completed as part of **EE463 – Power Electronics I**.  
The assignments focus on analytical modeling, simulation-based analysis, and design-oriented problem solving in power electronics.

## Covered Topics

### Homework 1 – Diode Rectifiers
- Single-phase half-wave and full-bridge diode rectifiers  
- Three-phase diode rectifiers  
- Average output voltage, input current THD, and power factor  
- Harmonic analysis and impact of line inductance and DC-link capacitance  

### Homework 2 – Power Semiconductor Devices and DC–DC Converters
- Power diode and MOSFET parameters and loss mechanisms  
- Technology comparison (Si, SiC) and design trade-offs  
- Buck and boost converter analysis and simulation  
- CCM boundary conditions, ripple analysis, inrush current, and efficiency  

### Homework 3 – Isolated DC–DC Converter Design
- Duty-controlled full-bridge isolated converter design  
- Passive component sizing and output ripple analysis  
- Transformer and inductor magnetic design  
- Copper and core loss estimation, thermal considerations, and non-ideal effects  

## Tools
- MATLAB / Simulink (Simscape Power Systems)  
- Analytical calculations and waveform analysis
